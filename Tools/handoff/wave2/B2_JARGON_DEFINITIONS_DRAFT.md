## Numerology category

- **Life Path** — your core number computed from your full birth date
- **Expression** — a number computed from the letters of your full name
- **Soul Urge** — a number from the vowels of your name; what a numerology tradition reads as inner motivation
- **Personality** — a number from the consonants of your name; what a numerology tradition reads as outer presence
- **Birthday** — the reduced single-digit value of your day of birth
- **Master Number** — in Western numerology, 11/22/33 are kept unreduced as standalone numbers
- **Master Builder (22)** — the conventional Western-numerology label for Personality 22
- **The Communicator (3)** / **The Liberator (5)** / **The Humanitarian (9)** — conventional Western-numerology archetype labels for these single-digit values
- **Pinnacle Cycles** — four life phases derived from birth-date math; each phase is one cycle
- **Attitude Number** — a number from your birth month and day; one numerology tradition reads it as first-impression style
- **Balance Number** — a number from the initials of your name; one numerology tradition reads it as how you steady yourself under pressure
- **Daily cycle index** — a single-day number from a modern cycle theory; "neutral" means your day's value sits at the midpoint
- **Personal Year / Month / Day** — calendar-based numerology cycles that change over time

## Name Intelligence category

- **Abjad** — the Arabic letter-numbering system (each letter has a numeric value)
- **Abjad Kabir** — the most common ("greater") Abjad numbering scheme
- **Gematria** — the Hebrew letter-numbering system (parallel to Abjad)
- **Mispar Gadol** — the most common ("greater value") Gematria scheme
- **Cognate sum** — the value when your Arabic letters are mapped onto another script's letter values; identical totals across traditions reflect this construction, not independent traditions arriving at the same answer (SIRR labels these MODERN_SYNTHESIS in the underlying registry)
- **Mandaean** — refers here to a modern synthesis of Mandaic letter values; the native Mandaic numeric system uses a duodecimal scheme that is computed separately
- **Ethiopian (cognate)** — Ge'ez letter values mapped onto Arabic input
- **Name Weight** — a SIRR composite metric that scores name length and letter rarity; the scale runs roughly 1-15 with most names landing 4-9
- **Notarikon** / **Albam** — Hebrew cipher transformations (acronyms / letter swap); applied here to Arabic input as a modern method
- **Birth Card (tarot)** — a tarot card derived from your birth date
- **Birth Card (cartomancy)** — a playing-card equivalent from a separate (cartomantic) tradition
- **Shadow Card** — a second tarot card paired with the birth card; one tradition reads it as compensatory pattern
- **Hidden Passion** — a Western numerology signal from letter frequency

## Pattern category

- **Threshold Birth** — a SIRR pattern triggered when your birth date falls between defined transition periods in multiple traditions
- **Bifurcated Roots** — a SIRR pattern triggered when your name-derived and birth-derived organizing numbers point in different directions
- **Barzakh** — Arabic for "isthmus" or "between-state"; the term appears in Ibn Arabi's Futuhat as a metaphysical category for liminal phases
- **Sandhi / Sandhya** — a Vedic Jyotish term for the junction between two periods (sunrise, sunset, two zodiacal sectors)
- **Liminal period** — anthropology's term for a transition phase between two stable identities or states
- **Split Crown** — a SIRR composite term, no single classical cognate, for the name/birth axis divergence pattern
- **Threshold moments** — life events that fall on a transition between defined phases in any tradition's frame
- **Organizing axis** — the through-line that pools across multiple measurements; what the synthesis returns to

## Astro Timing category

- **Ephemeris** — a precision astronomical table of planet positions; Swiss Ephemeris is the open-source reference set used by most professional astrology software
- **Swiss Ephemeris** — the open-source astronomical position library used by SIRR
- **Profection** — a Hellenistic timing technique that advances one house per year of life
- **6th House (Virgo when profected)** — the customer's profected house this year, in the sign that 6 houses past their natal ascendant points to. The Virgo here is the house's ruling sign, not the customer's sun sign
- **Firdaria** — a medieval Persian-Arabic technique that divides life into planet-ruled periods; periods 18-31 here means Mercury+Sun jointly rule those years
- **Mahadasha** — the major life period in the Vedic Vimshottari system; each runs 6-20 years
- **Vimshottari** — a Vedic 120-year planetary cycle that assigns each life portion a planetary ruler
- **Rahu** — the north lunar node in Vedic astrology; one tradition reads it as a multiplier of whatever it touches
- **Shravana** — one of 27 Vedic lunar mansions (nakshatras); your moon's nakshatra
- **Nakshatra** — Vedic lunar mansion (one of 27 sectors of the moon's path)
- **In Joy** — classical Hellenistic placement: a planet "in joy" sits in the house its tradition assigns it; Sun's joy is the 9th house
- **Birth Ruler** — the planet a given Vedic tradition treats as your dominant ruler at birth
- **BaZi** — the Chinese Four Pillars system; reads year, month, day, and hour as a four-pillar identity
- **Four Pillars** — the four time-based pillars of BaZi (year/month/day/hour)
- **Guǐ** — one of 10 Heavenly Stems in BaZi; corresponds to Yin Water
- **Day Animal / Year Animal** — Chinese zodiac assigns one animal to your year and one to your day; some lineages read year as public self, day as private self
- **Female Monkey (Vedic)** — Vedic compatibility tradition assigns one of 14 animal/gender pairings independent of the customer's actual gender
- **Vine (Celtic)** — Celtic tree calendar assigns one of 21 trees to a date range
- **Manik (Mayan)** — one of 20 day-signs in the Mayan tzolkin calendar; "12 Manik" pairs the day-sign with one of 13 numeric tones
- **Melancholic (humoral)** — one of four classical Greek temperaments; describes a tendency toward depth and reflection, not necessarily sadness
- **Chronobiology** — modern study of biological rhythms over hours, days, and seasons; the value here is a continuous index from a published model
- **Circadian organ clock** — a 24-hour mapping of organs to peak-activity hours used in Traditional Chinese Medicine

## Convergence + Statistics category

- **Convergence** — when independent systems land on the same number; SIRR counts how many systems agree and on which value
- **Independent families** — groups of traditions whose math is structurally distinct (vs traditions whose math derives from the same letter values)
- **System** — a single calculation method (e.g. Pinnacle Cycle 1, Soul Urge); a tradition typically contains multiple systems
- **Cultural group** — a high-level grouping (e.g. "Western numerology," "Vedic astrology") that contains multiple systems
- **Coherence score** — a SIRR meta-statistic: how clean the convergence pattern is. Higher = systems agree more sharply on fewer values; lower = signals are spread across many values
- **Divergent (in coherence)** — coherence score below the median; means your reading distributes attention across many values rather than clustering
- **Barzakh Coefficient** — a SIRR composite scoring how much your reading sits in liminal/threshold patterns; ranges 0-1, median ~0.5
- **Tradition-vote element** — a four-oracle vote on which classical element your reading leans toward; one evidence lane, not a competing answer to the synthesis element
- **Hermetic framing (axes)** — a SIRR comparative metric scoring placement on a hermetic correspondence axis
- **Cross-Scripture Intersection** — a SIRR pattern matcher across the structures of Quranic, Biblical, and Vedic texts; "pattern N" labels which structural rhyme matched
- **Monte Carlo** — a statistical method that runs the calculation on thousands of random inputs to establish what convergence looks like by chance; SIRR uses 10,000 random baselines
- **Random baseline** — the convergence pattern that appears when name+birth inputs are randomized; what your real result is being compared against
- **Lo Shu Grid / Nine Star Ki / Flying Star / Bazhai** — Chinese number-grid traditions that map birth date onto nine cells; each tradition reads the cell pattern differently
- **Tibetan Mewa** — the Tibetan version of the nine-star grid; "mewa" is Tibetan for "mole" or "spot"
- **Dwad** — Vedic 1/12th sub-divisional chart of your zodiac sign
- **Tarot Year Card** — a Western practice that derives one tarot card per calendar year from the birth date
- **Agrippan** — refers to Cornelius Agrippa's 16th-century Three Books of Occult Philosophy; SIRR uses one of his number-attribution tables
- **Hidden Passion** / **Luminous Dark** — SIRR pattern names; not classical terms

## Editorial notes

1. **Definitions deliberately avoid endorsing claims.** "Vimshottari is a Vedic 120-year planetary cycle that assigns each life portion a planetary ruler" describes what the tradition CLAIMS without claiming it's true. This matches the engineer-not-mystic register.

2. **Three terms have the most subtle definitions and bear careful reading**:
   - "Cognate sum" — the definition explicitly notes that identical totals across traditions reflect construction, not independent convergence. This is non-negotiable per the Grok Round 2 scholarship correction (Apr 16).
   - "Mandaean" — the definition distinguishes the cognate-mapped value from the native Mandaic duodecimal system, per the same correction.
   - "Tradition-vote element" — labeled as "one evidence lane, not a competing answer" to align with the Wave 2 reconciliation decision.

3. **Some terms (`Hidden Passion`, `Luminous Dark`, `Split Crown`, `Barzakh Coefficient`, `Name Weight`) are SIRR-coined.** Definitions say so explicitly. Avoiding the appearance of pseudo-classical authority.

4. **`Manik`** definition pairs the day-sign with a number — useful because the customer reads `MAYAN 12 Manik` and the 12 is otherwise unexplained.

5. **`Female Monkey` (Vedic)** definition addresses the "I'm not female" question that came up in 2 of 4 walks.

6. **Coverage gap to flag**: Several terms in `+more` collapsed lists (e.g. `flying_star`, `bazhai`, `agrippan`) appeared without context in the walks. Definitions cover them, but the rendered display is still a comma-separated list. Worth considering whether collapsed lists need a different rendering strategy than inline parens (probably do).

7. **~75 terms in this draft** — the brief estimated 40, but the walks surfaced more. We can ship a smaller set for Wave 2 (just the high-frequency terms) and add the rest later. My recommendation: ship all of them. The marginal cost is small once the inline-parenthetical pattern exists.
