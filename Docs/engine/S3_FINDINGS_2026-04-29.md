## TL;DR

The locked S3 bar (≤1.5% Tier-1 FP rate, ≤5% Tier-2) was set before measurement was actually run. Measurement now done. **The bar as currently specified is incompatible with the engine's current architecture.**

- 100% of random profiles produce ≥9 Tier-1 convergence events
- 22.83% of random profiles match life-path-to-dominant-root at 16+ systems
- Muhab's own profile peak (max_sys=22) sits BELOW the random-profile mean (25.08) — at the 26th percentile from the top

The engine emits convergence as a continuous statistical signal, not as a rare discrete event. The S3 bar assumes the latter. **Either the bar must be revised, or the architecture must be changed.** Both paths are honest; neither is "we just need to tune thresholds harder."

This document is the basis for that decision. Three options below. **Recommendation:** Path B (reframe to percentile honesty), with Path C (defer + monitor) as the aggressive-launch fallback.

**Implication:** at current thresholds (`min_systems=3`, `min_groups=2` for Tier-2 baseline; `min_groups=3` for Tier-1 Resonance; percentile boost for Tier-1 Significant), **every random profile produces between 9 and 17 Tier-1 convergence events.**

The discrete-event FP rate at current thresholds is **100%, not 1.5%.** To hit a 1.5% tail, the threshold for "any Tier-1 firing" would need to be raised to require `tier1_count ≥ 17` per profile — at which point only 0.13% of profiles trigger.

But Muhab's own profile has `tier1_count = 13` (median of the distribution). **At the 1.5% bar, Muhab's reading would show no Tier-1 convergence.** The product founder's canonical demo would not trigger the headline signal.

### Finding 3: Life-path-matches-dominant at 16+ systems is 22.83% on random

p_values from MC:
- `p(lp_matches_dominant)` = 0.1223 — 12.23% of random profiles have life-path matching dominant root (any system count)
- `p(lp_at_16_plus_systems)` = 0.2283 — 22.83% of random profiles have life-path matching dominant root with ≥16 systems agreeing

**Implication:** the convergence pattern that the reading frames as "your dominant root and life-path root align" appears in 22.83% of random profiles at the same threshold. If this is what "strong convergence" means in the reading, random data produces it ~15× more often than the 1.5% Tier-1 bar.

### Finding 4: Cognate-derived modules inflate apparent independence

Per Apr 16 Grok scholarship audit (already reflected in PR #50 customer-facing copy):
- `mandaean_gematria`, `ethiopian_asmat`, `hebrew_gematria` operate on Arabic input mapped to their script's letter values
- Their cross-tradition agreement with `arabic_name` group modules is identity-by-construction, not independent witnesses
- For S3 measurement: these modules contribute votes in their assigned groups but the resulting "convergence across X traditions" is computational, not historical

This was previously a customer-facing copy issue (PR #50 fixed). It is **also** a measurement issue: any FP rate that counts these modules as "independent traditions" overstates the convergence by 3 systems on average.

## The architectural mismatch

The engine's design is "more modules = stronger signal." More votes mean higher convergence counts. With 238 modules, even random profiles produce 9-17 Tier-1 events.

The S3 bar's design is "convergence is a rare discrete event." Random profiles should rarely trigger. Calibrated thresholds should make Tier-1 firing the exception.

These are incompatible:
- To make Tier-1 firing rare on random data, thresholds must be tightened so much that real customer profiles also rarely trigger (Muhab himself wouldn't trigger at the 1.5% bar)
- To preserve the customer experience of "you have a dominant signal," the engine must keep its current thresholds — meaning the headline signal fires on virtually any input

**This is not a bug. It is a design decision the engine makes implicitly that the calibration bar didn't reckon with.**

## Three options for resolution

### Path A — Tighten thresholds drastically (bar wins)

Raise `min_systems` and/or `min_groups` until ≤1.5% of random profiles produce any Tier-1 event. Concrete proposal: require `peak_count ≥ 36` AND `peak_groups ≥ 5` for "Primary Signal" to fire.

**Cost:** Customer reading shows "no primary signal" for ~98.98% of profiles. Including Muhab's own. Defeats the headline product.

**Honest about what:** Convergence is in fact rare under tightened thresholds. The 1.5% bar holds.

**Honest about what NOT:** Real customer experience of paying for a reading and getting "we don't see a primary signal in your profile." Most customers would feel the product failed.

**Recommendation:** Reject. Path A is rigorous but ships a worse product.

### Path B — Reframe to percentile honesty (architecture wins)

Drop the binary "Tier-1 fired vs didn't" framing. Replace with continuous percentile display: customer's peak count shown alongside its percentile in the n=10,000 baseline.

Proposed reading-page change:

```
{peak_count} traditions converge on your primary signal
across {peak_groups} tradition families
[your peak sits in the {percentile} of our reference baseline]
```

For Muhab: "22 traditions converge on your primary signal across 7 tradition families. Your peak sits in the lower-third of our reference baseline of 10,000 randomly-generated profiles."

For a customer with `max_sys=33`: "33 traditions converge on your primary signal across 9 tradition families. Your peak sits in the top 5% of our reference baseline."

**Cost:** Reading-page redesign. ~1-2 days of customer-visible copy work. Email + PDF report also need percentile context.

**Honest about what:** What the engine actually measures (peak position in distribution). No fictional "rare event" framing.

**Honest about what NOT:** Some customers will see "your peak is in the lower third" and feel the product told them their signal is weak. Mitigation: frame the lower percentile as "your structural pattern is in a common region — meaning many profiles share it" rather than "your signal is weak."

**Recommendation:** This is the rigorous engineering answer. It revises `S3_CALIBRATION_BAR.md` from "≤1.5% FP" to "peak-percentile delivery accuracy: customer's percentile must be within ±1% of MC baseline ground truth, n=10,000."

### Path C — Document, defer, ship as-is

Acknowledge the gap. Update `S3_CALIBRATION_BAR.md` with a "MEASURED: incompatible with current architecture" addendum. PRs #50 + #51 already softened customer-facing convergence framing — the reading no longer claims "independent witnesses agree." Defer architectural reframe (Path B) to post-launch when first-100-customer data lands.

**Cost:** 0 dev cost on customer surfaces. Documentation updates only.

**Honest about what:** The aggressive launch path is locked. The product framing is already honest about cognate-derived systems and computational convergence. The remaining gap (binary "fired" vs continuous percentile) is real but not blocking customer trust.

**Honest about what NOT:** Internal claim "we hit the 1.5% Tier-1 bar." We did not. We measured and documented why we didn't. Path C requires updating the bar to reflect this.

**Recommendation:** This is the aggressive-launch fallback. Per the audit plan's launch-gating guidance, "S3 must clear" — but "clear" can mean "measured and documented" rather than "FP ≤ 1.5%." This interpretation requires Muhab's explicit revision of the bar.

## What S3 cannot answer right now (deferred)

- **False-negative rate.** What fraction of profiles with genuinely-meaningful convergences (e.g., real historical figures with documented life patterns) get flagged by the engine vs missed? FN measurement requires real-world ground truth that doesn't exist yet.
- **Customer experience calibration.** Does the percentile framing land emotionally for paying customers? Requires real customer feedback. First-100-customer beta is the data source.
- **Cross-script identity-by-construction quantification.** What fraction of Tier-1 convergences arise from `mandaean_gematria` + `ethiopian_asmat` + `hebrew_gematria` (cognate-derived) vs genuinely-distinct traditions? Requires module-level disaggregation in the MC.

These are post-launch refinements. They do not block live-mode flip.
